POTATO

Basic Information

Potato is cultivated as a tuberous vegetable. India ranks third in the world in terms of potato production. Potato is very much liked in India, it is one such vegetable, which can be made with any vegetable. Potato is cultivated everywhere in India except in the states of Kerala and Tamil Nadu. Consumption of potato is beneficial for the body, but consuming too much of it can cause problems like increasing body fat.

Potato Cultivation Suitable Soil, Climate and Temperature

Potato cultivation requires fertile soil rich in organic matter. Its cultivation requires sandy loam soil with proper drainage. General P.H. It can be easily cultivated in the valued land. The temperate and tropical climate is considered suitable for potato cultivation. In India, it is cultivated in the winter season, but the frost falling in winter damages its plants.
Its fruits get spoiled even in hot climate. Because of which its plants need light rain. For good production of potato, normal temperature is required, high and low temperature harms its plants. Its plants can tolerate a maximum temperature of 25 degrees and a minimum of 15 degrees. Higher than this temperature is harmful for the plants.

Potato is cultivated in friable soil. For this, first deep plowing is done in the field with soil turning plows. After plowing the field is left open like this for a few days. After this, 15 carts of old cow dung manure or vermi compost is put in the field as natural manure and it is plowed again. Due to this, the manure of cow dung gets mixed well in the soil of the field. After this, the field is pulverized by applying water, after plowing, when the soil of the field starts appearing dry from above, Then DAP in the form of chemical fertilizer.
