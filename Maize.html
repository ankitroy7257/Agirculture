MAIZE

Basic Information

Maize is a major food crop, which comes under the category of coarse cereals. It is the form of a maize or corn. The states where maize is widely cultivated in India are Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Karnataka, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh etc. Out of these, Rajasthan has the largest area of ​​Maize and Andhra has the highest production. But the importance of maize is Jammu Kashmir, Himachal, Northeast states, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Maharashtra,

Seed Specification

Above land :- Maize can be cultivated in all types of land. But for good growth and productivity of maize, loamy and medium to heavy soil with adequate amount of fauna and proper drainage system is suitable. For this, such land where there is good drainage of water is suitable.

Field preparation: For the cultivation of maize, before sowing, plow the field thoroughly 2-3 times with a native plow or cultivator, so that the soil becomes friable, then after this, prepare the field for sowing by running the pad.

Famous varieties :- Hybrid Species - Ganga-1, Ganga-4, Ganga-11, Deccan-107, KH-510, DHM-103, DHM-109, Him-129, Pusa Early Ha-1 & 2, Vivek Ha-4, DHM- 15 etc. Composite Species - Narmada Moti, Jawahar Maize-216, Sandalwood Maize-1, 2 and 3, Sandalwood White Maize-2, Pusa Composite-1, 2 and 3, Mahi Kanchan, Arun, Kiran, Jawahar Maize-8, 12 and 216 , Prabhat, Navjot etc.

Fertilizers and Chemicals :- While preparing the land for the cultivation of maize, 5 to 8 tonnes of well-rotted cow dung should be mixed in the field and after soil testing, where there is a shortage of zinc, 25 kg/ha zinc sulphate should be applied before rain. Apply Phosphorus 75-150 kg, Urea 75-110 kg and Potash 15-20 kg per acre. Keep in mind that chemical fertilizers should be used only on the basis of soil test.
